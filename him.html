2. Daily Supplement Schedule

Supplement Dose Timing Notes
Collagen (hydrolysed) 15g 7:15am — 15 min pre-workout Mix in water. Pair immediately with 80mg Vitamin C (½ glass OJ or 1 kiwi). Vitamin C required for collagen synthesis. On rest days: skip.
Vitamin D3 4,000 IU With breakfast (~8:30am) Take with a fatty meal — D3 is fat-soluble.
Vitamin K2 MK-7 150mcg With breakfast alongside D3 Always pair K2 with D3 — directs calcium to bones, not arteries.
Creatine monohydrate 5g Post-workout with breakfast Mix into oats or milk. On rest days: take with breakfast instead. No loading phase needed after initial.
Omega-3 fish oil 2g EPA+DHA With dinner (~7:00pm) Take with food to reduce fishy burps. Check label — dose to achieve 2g combined EPA+DHA, not just total oil.
Zinc picolinate 30mg With dinner (~7:00pm) Picolinate form has good absorption. Do not take on empty stomach.
Magnesium glycinate 400mg 9:15pm with small snack Glycinate form: gentle, non-laxative, supports sleep quality and muscle recovery. Small snack = handful of nuts.
Whey protein 30g Post-workout mixed with 250ml cold milk Only use if daily protein from food falls short of 130g. Not mandatory if meals are on target.

Key rules:
- D3 + K2 always together, always with fat
- Collagen + Vitamin C always paired, always 15 min pre-workout (not on rest days)
- Magnesium at night — do not take in the morning, reduces sleep benefit
- Creatine timing is flexible; consistency matters more than exact window

Zone 2 target for age 42: Heart rate 107–125 bpm (180 − 42 = 138 max; Zone 2 = ~65–90% aerobic threshold). For rucking, aim for the lower end — pack weight will push HR up. You should be able to hold a conversation but feel consistently warm.

Why staying in Zone 2 matters — not Zone 3 or higher: Once HR climbs above ~125 bpm into Zone 3 (the "grey zone"), you shift from predominantly fat oxidation to carbohydrate burning, and the training stimulus changes entirely. Zone 3 is neither easy enough to recover from nor hard enough to build true cardiovascular capacity — it produces fatigue without the mitochondrial adaptations you want. For someone doing 4 hard strength sessions per week, a Zone 3+ ruck is an unplanned fifth hard session that accumulates systemic fatigue and impairs recovery for Monday's training. Zone 2 keeps the aerobic system developing (PGC-1α signalling, mitochondrial biogenesis, improved fat oxidation) while leaving the body fresh for strength work. If your HR spikes — uphill section, heavier pack, Singapore heat — slow your pace or pause briefly to let it drop back down.

5. The 4 Training Days — Detailed

Each session is 30 minutes: 2 min warm-up, ~22 min main work (superset pairs), 4–5 min prehab finisher. Supersets pair antagonist or non-competing movements — you rest one muscle group while working another, making 30 minutes as productive as a conventional 45-minute session. Rest: 45 sec between supersets (W1/W3/W4), 60 sec (W2 strength week).

Day A — Hinge + Push (Monday, 7:30–8:00am)

Purpose: Develop posterior chain power and strength via hip hinge; build horizontal push strength using floor press (shoulder-safe — floor limits ROM at the bottom, reducing impingement risk); balance the push with horizontal pull. Superset format pairs non-competing movements to maximise density in 30 minutes.

Movement patterns: Hinge, Horizontal Push, Horizontal Pull

Warm-up (2 min):
- Band pull-apart: 2×15 (shoulder prehab activation — do before any pressing)
- Shoulder CARs (Controlled Articular Rotations): 5 slow circles each arm

Superset A (4 rounds, 45s rest between rounds):

Exercise Cue / Key Technique Note
A1: KB Swing Hike it back (not squat down), explosive hip extension, glutes squeeze at top, soft landing back into hinge. This is a hip hinge, not a squat.
A2: DB Floor Press Lie on floor, DB in hands, elbows at 45° from torso (not flared). Floor stops the descent — this is the safety mechanism for your shoulder. Full lockout at top.

Superset B (4 rounds, 45s rest between rounds):

Exercise Cue / Key Technique Note
B1: DB Romanian Deadlift Soft knee bend, push hips back, DBs stay close to legs, feel stretch in hamstrings at bottom. Neutral spine throughout.
B2: Single-arm DB Row Knee and hand on floor. Pull elbow past hip. No shoulder elevation. Feel lat contraction at top.

Prehab Finisher (non-negotiable — 4 min):
- Band face pull: 2×15 — pull to forehead level, elbows high, thumbs back at end position
- Dead bug: 2×8/side — lower back pressed firmly into floor throughout; lower opposite arm/leg slowly

Day B — Squat + TGU (Tuesday, 7:30–8:00am)

Purpose: Build squat strength through full range; Turkish Get-Up (TGU) addresses shoulder stability and full-body integration — critical for long-term shoulder health. TGU is performed solo (cannot be superseted — it demands full focus). Goblet squat and farmer carry are paired as a metabolic superset.

Movement patterns: Shoulder Stability, Squat, Carry/Core

Warm-up (2 min):
- Glute bridge: 2×10 (activate glutes before squat)
- BW goblet squat: 2×8 (elbows inside knees at bottom, chest tall)

Turkish Get-Up — 3 sets × 2 reps/side (solo — TGU requires full focus, cannot be superseted; move slowly):

Exercise Cue / Key Technique Note
Turkish Get-Up (TGU) Do this first while completely fresh — it demands full concentration. Move slowly through each phase. Never rush a TGU. KB arm stays locked vertical throughout. Scan the KB at all times.

Superset A (4 rounds, 45s rest):

Exercise Cue / Key Technique Note
A1: KB Goblet Squat KB at chest, elbows up, chest tall. Sit into hips, knees track over toes. Full depth.
A2: KB Farmer Carry (20m) KB in one or both hands. Shoulders packed down and back. Tall posture, slight core brace. Do NOT lean to the loaded side.

Prehab Finisher (4 min):
- Band face pull: 2×15 — pull to forehead level, elbows high, thumbs back at end position
- RKC Plank: 2×20 sec — tuck pelvis, squeeze glutes, pull elbows toward toes, pull toes toward elbows. Total body tension.

Day C — Single-Leg + Power (Thursday, 7:30–8:00am)

Purpose: Single-leg strength for athletic balance and injury prevention; power output via KB swing; push-up kept at RPE 6 max for shoulder safety; anti-lateral carry for core stability. Superset format pairs non-competing movements.

Movement patterns: Hinge/Power, Single-Leg Squat, Push (RPE 6 max), Carry

Warm-up (2 min):
- Band Y-T-W: 2×8 each position — do this before any pressing; activates lower traps and rotator cuff
- Y: arms at ~135° (overhead Y shape), thumbs up
- T: arms at 90° (T shape), thumbs up
- W: elbows bent, pull band, squeeze shoulder blades
- Shoulder CARs: 5 each arm

Superset A (4 rounds, 45s rest):

Exercise Cue / Key Technique Note
A1: KB Swing Power opener — hike it back, explosive hip extension, glutes squeeze at top. Sets the explosive tone.
A2: DB Reverse Lunge Step back (not forward — easier on knee). Knee hovers 1–2cm above floor. Front foot stays flat. Control the descent.

Superset B (3 rounds, 45s rest):

Exercise Cue / Key Technique Note
B1: Push-up variation (RPE 6 max) Choose by current capacity: Standard → Close-grip → Archer push-up. No heavy overhead loading. If shoulder feels off, go one step easier. Never push through discomfort.
B2: KB Suitcase Carry (20m) KB in ONE hand only. Walk without leaning to the KB side — this is the entire challenge. Anti-lateral flexion = obliques, QL, quadratus. Switch hands midway.

Prehab Finisher (4 min):
- Band Y-T-W: 2×8 each position (all three in sequence = 1 round)
- Bird dog: 2×6/side — opposite arm + leg extended, hold 2 sec at top, return without twisting hips

Day D — Full Body + Power (Friday, 7:30–8:00am)

Purpose: Full-body integration to end the training week; maximise KB power output; conditioning element to complement the strength work. Finish strong heading into the weekend. Heavier swings and 60s rest in Superset A to allow for maximal power output.

Movement patterns: Power, Squat, Pull, Push

Warm-up (2 min):
- Band pull-apart: 2×15
- Hip circles: 10 each direction

Superset A (4 rounds, 60s rest — heavier, more power):

Exercise Cue / Key Technique Note
A1: KB Swing (heaviest KB, maximal power) Focus on maximal hip power each rep. These should feel fast and explosive, not grind-y. Hip hinge, not a squat.
A2: Bulgarian Split Squat Rear foot elevated on sofa/sturdy chair ~45cm. DB in each hand. Lower until rear knee hovers above floor. Front shin stays near vertical. This will be humbling — that is correct.

Superset B (3 rounds, 45s rest):

Exercise Cue / Key Technique Note
B1: KB Single-arm Clean + Press (press at RPE 6 max) Clean to rack, brief pause, then press. Press at RPE 6 max — strict shoulder-awareness. If any impingement sensation, stop the press and finish set as clean-only.
B2: DB Bent-over Row Hinge to ~45°, DB in both hands. Row to hip crease, squeeze shoulder blades at top. Heavier than single-arm rows — bilateral advantage.

Core Finisher (3 min):
- Dead bug: 2×10/side — lower back pressed firmly into floor throughout
- Side plank: 2×20s/side — straight body alignment, top hip not drooping

6. 4-Week Undulating Periodisation

The principle: Intensity and volume rotate week-to-week across four distinct phases. This stops the body from accommodating to a fixed stimulus — a common plateau for advanced trainees. Movements stay constant; the stress variable changes.

Week Phase Superset Rounds Reps RPE Target Rest Between Supersets Notes
Week 1 Accumulation 4 rounds 8 7 45 sec Re-establish all movement patterns. Choose moderate weights. Track every weight used — this is your baseline.
Week 2 Strength 4 rounds 6 8 60 sec Increase load from Week 1. Fewer reps, more weight, longer rest. Supersets limit recovery vs straight sets — 6 reps at RPE 8 is the right target.
Week 3 Volume 3 rounds 12 7–8 45 sec Lighter than Week 2, but more total reps per set. Metabolic stress accumulates through superset density.
Week 4 Deload 2 rounds 8 5–6 45 sec 50% of Week 3's total volume. Same exercises, same form. Intentionally easy. This is where adaptation happens.

Cycle restart: After Week 4, return to Week 1 structure — but with heavier weights than the previous cycle's Week 1. This is progressive overload in action.

TGU exception: TGU does not follow the superset format and is performed solo. Sets and reps do not follow the main periodisation table:
- W1/W3: 3 sets × 2 reps/side
- W2: 3 sets × 2 reps/side — heavier KB, full concentration
- W4: 2 sets × 2 reps/side — light KB, quality only

8. Progressive Overload Rules

When to Increase Load

Increase weight on an exercise when both of the following are true:
1. You completed all sets at the top of the rep range (e.g. all 8 reps in W1, all 5 in W2) with good form
2. RPE was ≤7 on the final set for 2 consecutive sessions of that exercise

Increments:
- Dumbbells: +2kg per DB (i.e. +4kg total load for bilateral exercises)
- Kettlebells: next size up (typically +4kg jumps — if the jump feels too large, split the difference using a resistance band as counterbalance or add a DB)
- Push-ups: advance one variation level (standard → close-grip → archer → pike)

When NOT to Increase Load

  • Form deteriorates at any rep before the prescribed rep count
  • RPE is 8+ across all sets (not just the last one)
  • During deload week — no increases regardless of how easy it feels
  • Any session where shoulder feels pre-symptomatic (even slightly "off")

The Shoulder Rule

This is non-negotiable and applies to every pressing and overhead movement:

  1. If you feel shoulder discomfort (not muscle burn — discomfort) at any point during a press: stop that set immediately
  2. Drop to the previous weight or a lower push-up variation for the remainder of the session
  3. Add 1 extra set of band face pulls (3×15 → 4×15) at the prehab finisher for that session
  4. Do not push through impingement — training through shoulder pain converts a minor issue into a structural problem. One skipped set costs nothing. One impingement flare-up costs 2–4 weeks.

9. What to Track

Training Log (Every Session)

Keep a simple log — notebook, phone note, or app. Record after each session:

Field What to Write

Session e.g. Day A
Exercise Name
Weight used e.g. 2×18kg DB RDL

Why this matters: Without logs, "I think I used 18kg last time" becomes your planning basis. With logs, you know exactly what to beat.

Weekly Check-In (Same Day Each Week — Recommended: Sunday Morning)

  • [ ] Bodyweight: Morning, post-void, before eating. Record in kg to 1 decimal. Same scale, same conditions.
  • [ ] Waist measurement: Around navel level. Relaxed (not sucked in). 1 measurement per week.
  • [ ] Sleep quality: Subjective 1–5 (helps correlate recovery with training output)
  • [ ] Energy levels: Subjective 1–5

Bodyweight target over 4 weeks: +0.5 to +1.0kg. This is a lean surplus — you are not trying to gain fast; slow gain preserves the lean frame and avoids excess fat. If weight is unchanged after 2 weeks, add 100–150 kcal/day (extra tablespoon peanut butter, handful of nuts, or extra half-cup rice).

Shoulder Red Flags — Stop and Reassess

  • Sharp pain at any point during pressing movement
  • Pain that lingers more than 5 minutes post-set
  • Clicking/catching sensation under the acromion with overhead reach
  • Weakness on one side that was not present previously

If any of the above: skip remaining press sets that session, double the face pull volume, and if symptoms persist for >3 days, rest the shoulder completely for 5–7 days before returning to press movements.

Rucking Zone 2 Reference

Metric Target
Heart rate 107–125 bpm
Pace feel Can speak full sentences; breathing elevated but not gasping
Duration 35–45 min
Pack weight 8–10kg
Terrain Flat to moderate — avoid steep hills; inclines spike HR above Zone 2
Footwear Trail shoes or sturdy walking shoes — not running flats

Why you must not let HR spike above 125 bpm during rucking:

Zone 2 (107–125 bpm) is where the body trains the aerobic energy system most efficiently — it stimulates mitochondrial biogenesis (growth of new mitochondria in muscle cells) and develops the fat oxidation pathway. These are the long-term adaptations that improve cardiovascular health, metabolic efficiency, and longevity.

The moment HR crosses into Zone 3 (roughly 126–138 bpm), several things happen:
- Fuel source shifts — the body moves away from fat oxidation toward carbohydrate burning. You are no longer training the same system.
- Recovery cost rises sharply — Zone 3 is metabolically expensive. Unlike Zone 2, which you can recover from overnight, Zone 3 work accumulates fatigue over days. With 4 strength sessions already in the week, this erodes your capacity to train hard on Monday.
- No additional aerobic benefit — Zone 3 is sometimes called the "grey zone" because it is too easy to build VO2max (you need Zone 4–5 for that) and too hard to build aerobic base. It is the least productive intensity for your goals.

Practical control during Singapore rucking:
- If you hit a hill, slow your pace or take the hill at half-speed
- If HR spikes above 125, stop walking briefly (30–60 sec) until it drops back to 115, then resume
- In the morning heat (even at 7am), HR runs 5–10 bpm higher than in air-conditioning — factor this in and be willing to go slower than feels necessary
